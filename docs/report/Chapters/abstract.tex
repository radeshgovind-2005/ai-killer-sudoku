This work presents four automatic solvers for the classic Sudoku puzzle and its Killer Sudoku variant. In Prolog, depth-first iterative deepening (DFID) and A* were implemented, both with the minimum remaining values (MRV) heuristic. In Python, simulated annealing (SA) and a genetic algorithm (GA) were implemented using a within-box swap representation. All solvers were extended to support Killer Sudoku cage constraints. Experimental results show that DFID with MRV solves all instances in under four seconds. A* expands more nodes on puzzles with high degrees of freedom. SA reliably solves easy and medium instances but occasionally gets trapped in local minima at cost two. The GA suffers from premature convergence and does not find complete solutions within the tested parameter limits.
